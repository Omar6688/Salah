Biography

Mohamed Salah Hamed Mahrous Ghaly, born 15 June 1992 in Nagrig, Egypt , is an Egyptian footballer who plays for Liverpool . Since 2011, Salah also plays for the Egyptian national team . Salah has won the BBC African Footballer of the Year as well as the CAF African Football Player of the Year in both 2017 and 2018. After Salah's fantastic season with Liverpool in the Premier League 2017/2018 , he won, among other things, the PFA Player of the Year , PFA Fan's Player of the Year , PWA Player of the Year and was named the best player of the season in Liverpool. With 32 goals in 36 games, he also won the Premier League Golden Boot , and also broke the record for most goals scored in a season in the Premier League ever. The next season in the Premier League (2018/19) he scored 22 goals and again won the Premier League Golden Boot, but had to share the win with his teammate Sadio Mané and Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang who both scored as many goals. In 2018, he was ranked by FIFA as the third best football player in the world by their annual Best FIFA Football Player award . Salah also won the 2018 Fifa Puskás Award , for his goal in December 2017 against Everton in the Premier League. In 2018/19, Salah won the Champions League with Liverpool, and among other things scored the 1–0 goal in the final against Spurs , which Liverpool won 2–0.

Club Career

Salah started his senior playing in the Egyptian league club Arab Contractors in the years 2010-2012. In 2012, he was bought by FC Basel for 2.5 million euros. With Basel, he won the Swiss league in the 2012/2013 season . In January 2014, a transfer to Premier League club Chelsea took place . In February 2015, Chelsea loaned Salah to Italian side Fiorentina . Salah was later loaned out to AS Roma which later became a permanent deal, where he later stayed between 2015 and 2017. In the summer of 2017, he was sold to Liverpool for approximately 42 million euros. After joining Liverpool, he took his team to the 2018 Champions League Final , where they lost to Real Madrid 3-1. On 1 July 2022, the contract with Liverpool was extended.

National team career

Representing Egypt, Mohamed Salah has been pivotal for the national team. He played a key role in leading Egypt to the 2018 FIFA World Cup, their first appearance in 28 years, scoring crucial goals in qualification. Salah also shone in the Africa Cup of Nations, finishing as a runner-up in 2017 and helping the team reach the final in 2021. Known for his determination and leadership, Salah has become Egypt's all-time top scorer in World Cup qualifiers and a national hero, inspiring millions with his performances and unyielding commitment to the team.
